\chapter*{Abstract}
Course scheduling for first-years at Reed College is made uniquely complicated
by the year-long Humanities 110 requirement. Many first-years struggle to
schedule the same year-long section into both semesters amidst all their other
classes. Though there are many pre-existing resources
for course scheduling, they are expensive and generally targeted towards larger
institutions. Here, we work with The Reed College Registrar to develop a specialized
algorithm for Humanities 110 section assignment as a potential solution to this
first-year problem. Our algorithm is based on extensive research into stable
school choice and group fair course assignment algorithms, combining the work to
generate a mechanism for stable bipartite one-sided matching under
incomplete preferences and group fairness constraints. We compare our algorithm
to two other scheduling algorithms in experimental trials, and the results show
that our algorithm is a viable solution that could realistically be implemented
at Reed.
